Galaxies

Galaxies are combinations of millions and billions of stars.       
 Based on their appearance galaxies can be of following types.


Types of galaxies

Spiral galaxies:
These are the most common type of galaxies in the universe. .They consist of a rotating disc that contains stars ,dust, gas and a bulge or nucleus in the center.
Bulge is a consecration in globular clusters that orbit the core of the galaxy.
Ex: Andromeda Galaxy

Elliptical  galaxies:
These galaxies have an elliptical shape, hence the name and do not contain bulge.
Ex: ESO 325-G004

Irregular  galaxies:
They do not have definite shape but are in constant motion.
There are three types of irregular galaxies [Irr-I, Irr - II, Dlrr-III].

Barred spiral  galaxies:
They have similar features of spiral galaxies along with a bar of bright stars.
These bar of stars lie along the center of bulge or nucleus and extend into the disc.
There are three types of barred galaxies [Type A, B and C].
Ex: Milky way Galaxy

Andomeda Galaxy

It is the closest large spiral galaxy visible from the earth .It is located at about 24 lakh 80 thousand light years from earth with a diameter of about 2 lakh light years.

Our Galaxy
Milky Way Galaxy

It is a barred spiral galaxy with a diameter of 1 to 2 lakh light years .It is estimated to contain 100 to 400 billion stars. It appears as a hazy band of white light from the earth.
